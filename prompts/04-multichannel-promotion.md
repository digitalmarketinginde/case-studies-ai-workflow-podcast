# 📣 Prompt System: Multichannel Episode Promotion
## From Episode Script to Platform-Native Announcements

> **Philosophy:** Each platform has its own language, rhythm, and audience contract.
> The same episode announcement rewritten for each platform — not copy-pasted.
> Community-first tone: we speak TO people who already trust us, not AT cold audiences.

## Prompt 1 — Instagram & TikTok Stories

    You are writing a Stories announcement for a marketing podcast
    with an established, trust-based community of Ukrainian entrepreneurs and marketers.

    Episode title: [TITLE]
    Key insight from episode: [ONE SENTENCE]
    Target audience: [AUDIENCE]

    Write 3 Stories slides in sequence. Each slide = one short text block.

    SLIDE 1 — HOOK (max 7 words):
    A single provocative question or bold claim.
    Must create immediate "wait, what?" reaction.
    Examples of the RIGHT tone: "Ти витрачаєш бюджет не туди."
    "Я перестала боятися конкурентів. Ось чому."
    Do NOT write: "Новий епізод подкасту вже доступний!"

    SLIDE 2 — TEASE (3–4 lines):
    What will they learn? But reveal only enough to create FOMO.
    Write as if you are texting a friend who asked "так про що там?"
    Include the strongest non-obvious insight from the episode.
    End with an unfinished thought that forces them to swipe or click.

    SLIDE 3 — CTA with links (2 lines max):
    One direct invitation. Conversational, not corporate.
    Example: "Слухай де зручно 👇"
    YouTube: [LINK]
    Spotify: [LINK]
    Apple Podcasts: [LINK]

    Tone rules:
    - Ukrainian language, conversational register
    - No hashtags on Stories (they look desperate)
    - No emoji overload — maximum 2 emoji total across all slides
    - Sounds like a message from someone the audience already follows and trusts

## Prompt 2 — LinkedIn Post

    You are writing a LinkedIn post for a Marketing Director and CMO-level strategist
    sharing a new podcast episode with a professional Ukrainian and international audience.

    Episode title: [TITLE]
    Episode description: [DESCRIPTION]
    Key insight: [ONE SENTENCE]
    Target audience: [AUDIENCE]

    Write a LinkedIn post following this exact structure:

    LINE 1 — HOOK (standalone, no context):
    A bold professional observation or counterintuitive statement.
    Must stop the scroll in a professional feed.
    Must relate to a real pain point the target audience experiences daily.
    Example format: "Більшість маркетологів оптимізують не те."

    LINES 2–4 — SETUP (3 short paragraphs, 2–3 lines each):
    Build the argument. Share the professional insight from the episode.
    Use first person — this is a personal professional reflection, not a press release.
    Include one specific real-world observation from your 12+ years of experience.
    No generic industry commentary. No "research shows that..."

    LINE 5 — PIVOT:
    One line that connects the insight to the episode.
    Example: "Я розібрала це детально в новому епізоді."
    Do NOT write: "Радий поділитися своїм новим подкастом!"

    LINES 6–7 — VALUE PROMISE:
    What specific thing will the reader understand after listening?
    Be concrete. Name the technique, framework, or shift in thinking.

    CTA + LINKS:
    Слухай тут:
    YouTube: [LINK]
    Spotify: [LINK]
    Apple Podcasts: [LINK]

    HASHTAGS (5–7, end of post):
    Mix professional Ukrainian tags with reach tags.
    Always include: #маркетинг #подкаст #ідеївголос

    Tone rules:
    - Ukrainian language, professional but personal register
    - First person throughout — never "author" or third person
    - Zero corporate speak: no "leverage", "synergy", "holistic approach"
    - Post length: 150–220 words total
    - Line breaks after every 1–2 sentences — LinkedIn punishes walls of text

## Prompt 3 — Facebook Post

    You are writing a Facebook post for a community of Ukrainian entrepreneurs
    and marketers who follow a trusted marketing expert.

    Episode title: [TITLE]
    Episode description: [DESCRIPTION]
    Key insight: [ONE SENTENCE]
    Target audience: [AUDIENCE]

    Write a Facebook post following this structure:

    OPENING (2–3 lines):
    Start mid-thought — as if continuing a conversation already happening.
    Reference a universal frustration or experience the audience recognizes immediately.
    Example: "Знаєте той момент, коли витратив на рекламу — і тиша?"

    MIDDLE (3–4 lines):
    Share the episode's core insight as a personal realization or story.
    Make it feel like you discovered something and cannot wait to share it.
    One concrete example. One strong opinion.

    BRIDGE TO EPISODE (1 line):
    Natural, not salesy. "Записала цілий епізод про це — бо тема не відпускає."

    CTA + LINKS:
    Слухай де зручно:
    🎬 YouTube: [LINK]
    🎧 Spotify: [LINK]
    🍎 Apple Podcasts: [LINK]

    Tone rules:
    - Warm, direct, community-first
    - Feels like a post from a friend who happens to be an expert
    - No formal announcements — this is not a press release
    - Length: 80–120 words
    - 1–2 emoji maximum, only where they add warmth not decoration

## Prompt 4 — Telegram Channel Post

    You are writing a Telegram channel post for a Ukrainian marketing community
    that expects sharp, no-fluff insights delivered directly.

    Episode title: [TITLE]
    Key insight: [ONE SENTENCE]
    Target audience: [AUDIENCE]
    Transcript excerpt (strongest 2–3 minutes of episode): [PASTE EXCERPT]

    Write a Telegram post following this structure:

    OPENING PUNCH (1 line):
    The most provocative or surprising insight from the episode.
    Telegram audiences scroll fast — this line must stop them cold.
    No introduction. No "привіт". Start with the idea.

    BODY (4–6 short lines):
    Unpack the insight. Be direct. Use short sentences.
    Telegram readers want density — one idea per sentence.
    Include one concrete example or real number from the episode.
    End with a tension-creating observation that the episode resolves.

    EPISODE LINK BLOCK:
    Новий епізод: [EPISODE TITLE]
    ▶️ YouTube: [LINK]
    🎧 Spotify: [LINK]
    🍎 Apple: [LINK]

    Tone rules:
    - Sharpest, most direct tone across all platforms
    - No emoji as decoration — only as functional markers (▶️ 🎧)
    - No hashtags in Telegram posts (ineffective, looks unprofessional)
    - Length: 60–90 words maximum
    - Every sentence must earn its place

## Prompt 5 — Threads Post

    You are writing a Threads post for a Ukrainian marketing expert
    sharing a sharp professional observation with a growing audience.

    Episode title: [TITLE]
    Key insight: [ONE SENTENCE]
    Target audience: [AUDIENCE]

    Write a Threads post following this structure:

    POST (3–5 lines maximum):
    Threads rewards brevity and intellectual sharpness.
    Start with a single strong claim or observation.
    Add 1–2 lines of reasoning.
    End with an open question OR a statement that invites disagreement.
    The goal: comments, not clicks. Engagement before conversion.

    FOLLOW-UP REPLY (optional thread continuation):
    One additional line with the episode link.
    "Розгорнула цю думку в подкасті, якщо хочеш глибше:"
    [YOUTUBE LINK]

    Tone rules:
    - Most conversational and intellectually provocative tone
    - Sounds like a real thought, not a promotional post
    - No hashtags (Threads algorithm does not prioritize them)
    - Length: 40–60 words maximum for the main post

## Prompt 6 — YouTube Community Post

    You are writing a YouTube Community post for a podcast channel
    announcing a new episode to subscribers who already know and trust the content.

    Episode title: [TITLE]
    Episode description: [DESCRIPTION]
    Key insight: [ONE SENTENCE]
    Timestamp of the strongest episode moment: [e.g. 23:40]

    Write a YouTube Community post following this structure:

    OPENING QUESTION OR CLAIM (1 line):
    Ask the community something directly related to the episode topic.
    This is not rhetorical — you genuinely want their answer in comments.
    Example: "Скільки відсотків маркетинг-бюджету ти б виділив на контент?"

    EPISODE TEASE (3–4 lines):
    What's in the episode. But make it feel like insider access.
    Mention the timestamp of the best moment: "На [XX:XX] — момент, після якого
    переосмислила весь свій підхід до..."
    This drives timestamp-specific engagement which boosts the algorithm.

    CTA (1 line):
    Direct and simple. "Дивись новий епізод — посилання в описі каналу."

    Tone rules:
    - Most intimate tone — this is your core community
    - Speak to them as regular viewers, not new visitors
    - Length: 60–80 words
    - End with a question to drive comments

## Master Tone Rules (Apply to All Platforms)

These rules override any platform-specific instruction if there is conflict:

    COMMUNITY FIRST:
    Every post assumes the reader already knows and trusts the podcast.
    We are not convincing strangers. We are inviting friends.

    NO AI-SLOP PHRASES — eliminate immediately if generated:
    "Радий поділитися" → delete
    "Цікавий контент" → delete
    "Корисна інформація" → delete
    "Не пропусти" → replace with specific reason not to miss it
    "Підписуйся на наш подкаст" → replace with "Слухай де зручно"

    SPECIFICITY RULE:
    Every post must contain at least one specific detail
    that could only come from THIS episode — not any marketing podcast.
    A specific number, a specific mistake, a specific client scenario.

    THE TRUST TEST:
    Before finalizing any post, ask:
    "Would someone who already listens to this podcast feel that
    this post respects their intelligence and time?"
    If not — rewrite.

## Platform Publishing Sequence

Publish in this order for maximum organic reach:

    1. YouTube (upload + Community post) — primary platform
    2. Spotify + Apple Podcasts (audio distribution)
    3. LinkedIn (professional reach, drives consulting leads)
    4. Telegram (highest open rate, core community)
    5. Instagram Stories (visual discovery, younger audience)
    6. TikTok Stories (algorithmic reach, new audience)
    7. Facebook (community warmth, referral traffic)
    8. Threads (intellectual engagement, growing platform)
