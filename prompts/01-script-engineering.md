# 📝 Prompt System: Podcast Script Engineering
## From Raw Expert Voice to Structured Long-Form Script

> **Philosophy:** Expert thinking comes first. AI structures it — never replaces it.
> If a sentence can be generated by ChatGPT in one prompt — it is rejected.

---

## INPUT REQUIRED BEFORE STARTING

Prepare these before running any prompt:
- Raw voice transcript (recorded in Ukrainian, unscripted)
- Episode topic validated via Google Trends / Ahrefs / YouTube Analytics
- Target episode length: 40 min or 60 min
- 1–2 examples of your strongest previous episodes (for voice reference)

---

## Prompt 1 — Topic Validation & Clickability Test

    You are a senior content strategist specializing in Ukrainian-language marketing podcasts.

    I need to validate this podcast topic idea: [TOPIC IDEA]

    Analyze and deliver:

    1. TREND SIGNAL:
       Is this topic growing or declining in search interest right now?
       What is the estimated search demand in Ukrainian-speaking markets?

    2. CLICKABILITY SCORE:
       Rate the topic's clickability 1–10.
       Explain exactly what makes it click-worthy or why it falls flat.

    3. COMPETITIVE GAP:
       What are the top 3 existing pieces of content on this topic?
       What expert angle or opinion do they all miss?
       This gap is where my episode must live.

    4. TITLE OPTIONS — generate 3 variants:
       - EMOTIONAL: triggers fear, curiosity, or strong recognition
       - SEO: primary keyword in first 3 words, under 60 characters
       - BOLD CLAIM: a strong opinionated statement the viewer wants to challenge or confirm

    5. KEYWORD RECOMMENDATION:
       Primary keyword (highest demand, most relevant)
       3 secondary keywords (long-tail, lower competition)
       Which keyword to prioritize in the YouTube title?

    Language: Ukrainian
    Audience: entrepreneurs and digital marketers, Ukrainian-speaking

## Prompt 2 — Research Best-in-Class Podcast Structure

    You are analyzing the top-performing marketing and business podcasts on YouTube.

    Study the 20 most-watched episodes in the marketing/business niche globally.

    Analyze and report:

    1. HOOK ANATOMY:
       How do the strongest episodes open their first 60 seconds?
       What specific trigger types appear most often?
       (pattern interrupt / bold claim / provocative question / shocking statistic)

    2. RETENTION MECHANICS:
       What techniques do they use to hold attention across 40–60 minutes?
       Where do they place mid-episode re-engagement triggers?
       What story structures repeat across successful episodes?

    3. TRANSITION PATTERNS:
       How do they move between sections without losing momentum?
       What verbal cues signal a new chapter without sounding robotic?

    4. OPINIONATED POSITIONING:
       How do the strongest hosts use controversial or debatable positions
       to create engagement and intellectual tension?

    Summarize the top 5 structural patterns I must apply to my episode.
    Be specific — give real examples from real episodes where possible.

## Prompt 3 — Script Engineering from Raw Voice (Voice-Preserving)

    You are a podcast script editor for "Ideas Out Loud" (Ідеї вголос) —
    a Ukrainian-language marketing podcast for entrepreneurs and digital marketers.

    Here is my raw voice transcript recorded in Ukrainian: [PASTE TRANSCRIPT]

    YOUR TASK — apply in this exact order:

    STEP 1 — PRESERVE completely:
    - My exact metaphors and comparisons — do not replace them with "better" ones
    - My speaking rhythm and natural sentence length
    - My personal examples and client stories — these are non-negotiable
    - My strong opinions and positions — even if they sound controversial
    - My Ukrainian vocabulary and phrasing style

    STEP 2 — IMPROVE structure only:
    - Add a strong opening hook (first 60 seconds must create urgency or curiosity)
    - Restructure the logical flow so each point builds on the previous
    - Add 2–3 mid-episode retention triggers ("and here is where it gets interesting...")
    - Add clear section transitions that feel natural, not scripted
    - Create a strong closing that leaves the listener with one actionable insight

    STEP 3 — GENERATE full script:
    - Target length: [40 minutes / 60 minutes] of spoken word
    - Average speaking pace: 130–140 words per minute
    - Therefore target word count: [5200–5600 words / 7800–8400 words]
    - Format: continuous prose with section markers in [BRACKETS]

    HARD RULES — never violate these:
    - Do NOT add generic marketing advice that exists in any textbook
    - Do NOT replace my analogies with "cleaner" AI-generated ones
    - Do NOT use neutral language where I used opinionated language
    - Do NOT add bullet lists — this is spoken word, not a blog post
    - Do NOT write a conclusion that summarizes what was just said

    Output: complete structured script in Ukrainian, preserving full author identity.

## Prompt 4 — Extend & Deepen Weak Sections

    Review the script you just generated.

    Apply this quality pass — in this exact sequence:

    1. IDENTIFY WEAK SECTIONS:
       Flag every section where:
       - The argument feels incomplete or under-supported
       - A personal example or case study is missing
       - The language sounds like it was written by AI, not spoken by a human
       - A strong opinion was softened into a neutral observation

    2. DEEPEN EACH WEAK SECTION:
       For each flagged section:
       - Add a real-world example or analogy that fits MY voice
       - Strengthen the argument with specific reasoning, not general claims
       - If a position was softened — restore the original opinion and sharpen it
       - Add the "why this matters to the listener specifically" layer

    3. TIMING CHECK:
       After all extensions, verify the total word count matches target:
       [5200–5600 words for 40 min / 7800–8400 words for 60 min]
       If short — identify which sections can be expanded further.
       If long — flag which sections are weakest and can be trimmed.

    4. FINAL VOICE CHECK:
       Read the opening 300 words and the closing 300 words.
       Do they sound like me — or like a polished AI rewrite?
       If AI rewrite — restore my original phrasing from the transcript.

    Output: revised full script with all changes applied and a brief change log
    noting what was extended and why.

## Prompt 5 — Anti-AI-Slop Final Refinement Pass

    This is the final quality gate before the script goes to recording.

    Apply these strict editorial rules — no exceptions:

    RULE 1 — THE ONE-PROMPT TEST:
    Read every paragraph. Ask: "Could ChatGPT generate this exact paragraph
    if given only the topic name?"
    If YES — rewrite it using only content from my original voice transcript.
    The paragraph must contain something that exists only in my experience.

    RULE 2 — GENERIC PHRASE ELIMINATION:
    Find and replace every instance of:
    - "в сучасному світі" → replace with a specific year, trend, or data point
    - "ефективний підхід" → replace with the specific approach named precisely
    - "важливо розуміти" → replace with WHY it matters, not that it matters
    - "багато компаній" → replace with a specific company or client type
    - "як відомо" → delete entirely, start with the actual insight

    RULE 3 — OPINION RESTORATION:
    Find every place where a strong original opinion was softened.
    Examples of softened language to reverse:
    "деякі маркетологи вважають" → "я вважаю, і ось чому я правий"
    "є різні підходи" → "більшість підходів не працюють, і ось який працює"
    Restore the original opinionated voice.

    RULE 4 — THE LISTENER VALUE TEST:
    For each major section, answer: "What can the listener DO differently
    after hearing this that they could not do before?"
    If the answer is nothing — the section needs a concrete takeaway added.

    RULE 5 — HUMAN VOICE FINAL CHECK:
    The script must pass this test:
    "Would a listener prefer this over asking ChatGPT directly?"
    If not — that section needs a personal story, a real failure, or a strong opinion
    that only comes from 12+ years of real marketing experience.

    Output: final production-ready script in Ukrainian.
    Add a brief editor's note: list the 3 most important changes made in this pass.

---

## Self-Review Checklist Before Recording

After all 5 prompts are complete, verify the script manually:

- [ ] Opening hook creates genuine curiosity within 30 seconds
- [ ] Every key point contains a personal opinion or real experience
- [ ] Zero "10 tips" or list-style sections — all narrative
- [ ] At least one strong debatable position the listener might disagree with
- [ ] Zero generic phrases that could appear in any marketing blog
- [ ] Word count matches target timing
- [ ] The closing leaves ONE actionable insight — not a summary

---

## Recording Note

The final script is a guide, not a teleprompter.
The speaker is encouraged to:
- Rephrase sentences that feel unnatural when spoken aloud
- Add spontaneous insights that emerge during recording
- Skip or compress sections that lose energy in delivery
- Improvise examples that feel more relevant in the moment

**The script exists to give structure. The recording exists to give life.**
