# 📝 Prompt System: Podcast Script Engineering
## From Raw Expert Voice to Structured Long-Form Script

> **Philosophy:** Expert thinking comes first. AI structures it — never replaces it.

---

## Stage 1 — Topic Discovery & Validation

Before any recording, the topic is validated using a hybrid research system.

### Tools Used

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Google Trends | Search demand signals, trend momentum |
| Ahrefs | Keyword volume + competition analysis |
| YouTube Analytics | Top-performing content in niche |
| ChatGPT / Claude / Perplexity | Idea expansion, angle framing, clickability testing |

### Prompt 1.1 — Topic Brainstorm & Clickability Test

    You are a senior content strategist specializing in marketing podcasts.
    
    I need to validate a podcast topic idea: [TOPIC IDEA]
    
    Analyze:
    1. Is this topic trending right now? What is the search demand signal?
    2. What is the clickability potential? Rate 1-10 and explain why.
    3. Suggest 5 alternative angles of this topic that would perform better on YouTube.
    4. What are the top 3 competing pieces of content on this topic? What do they miss?
    5. Formulate 3 title options: one emotional, one SEO-optimized, one curiosity-driven.
    
    Language: Ukrainian
    Audience: Ukrainian-speaking entrepreneurs and digital marketers

### Prompt 1.2 — Keyword Strength Analysis

    Analyze keyword demand for the podcast topic: [TOPIC]
    
    Provide:
    - Primary keyword (highest volume, relevant)
    - 5 secondary keywords (long-tail, lower competition)
    - Search intent classification (informational / navigational / commercial)
    - Recommended keyword to prioritize in YouTube title
    
    Language: Ukrainian

---

## Stage 2 — Raw Expert Voice Capture

The author records multiple long-form voice notes in Ukrainian — unscripted, raw thinking.

**What to capture:**
- Main argument or position on the topic
- Personal experience and real client cases
- Analogies and metaphors that feel natural
- Spontaneous insights and strong opinions
- "Why this matters" — emotional reasoning

> Rule: No scripting at this stage. Raw thinking only.

---

## Stage 3 — AI Script Engineering (3-Command Sequence)

### Prompt 3.1 — Research Best-in-Class Structure

    Study the top 20 most-watched marketing and business podcasts on YouTube.
    
    Analyze their tactics:
    - How do they open (hook structure in first 60 seconds)?
    - What clickbait triggers do they use without losing credibility?
    - How do they maintain attention across 40-60 minute episodes?
    - What storytelling patterns appear most frequently?
    
    Summarize the top 5 structural patterns I should use for my podcast episode.

### Prompt 3.2 — Structure My Script (Voice-Preserving)

    You are editing a podcast script for "Ideas Out Loud" — a Ukrainian-language marketing podcast.
    
    Here is my raw voice transcript: [PASTE TRANSCRIPT]
    
    Your task:
    1. PRESERVE completely: my author voice, my metaphors, my comparisons,
       my speaking rhythm, my examples, my opinions
    2. IMPROVE: logical structure, section transitions, hook strength, attention retention
    3. ADD: strong opening hook (first 60 seconds), mid-episode retention triggers,
       clear section markers
    4. GENERATE the full script within this timing: [40 minutes / 60 minutes]
    5. DO NOT: rewrite my ideas in generic language, remove my personal examples
    
    Output: Full structured podcast script in Ukrainian, preserving author identity.

### Prompt 3.3 — Extend & Deepen

    Improve and extend what you just generated.
    
    Focus on:
    - Sections where the argument feels weak — add depth and reasoning
    - Any section that sounds AI-generated — rewrite it to sound like an opinionated expert
    - Ensure total length matches [40 / 60] minute timing
    
    Do not add generic content. Every addition must feel like it came from
    a real expert with 12+ years of experience.

---

## Stage 4 — Anti-AI-Slop Refinement

### Self-Review Checklist

- [ ] Does every key point contain a personal opinion or real experience?
- [ ] Could this sentence be generated by ChatGPT in one prompt? If yes — rewrite it
- [ ] Are there any "10 tips" style lists? Replace with narrative
- [ ] Is there at least one strong, debatable position in the episode?
- [ ] Does the opening hook create genuine curiosity within 30 seconds?

### Prompt 4.1 — Final Refinement Pass

    Review this podcast script one final time.
    
    Apply these strict rules:
    - Remove ALL generic marketing advice (things anyone could Google)
    - Remove ALL neutral statements — replace with strong opinions
    - Ensure every section has at least one original insight not found elsewhere
    - Test: "Would a listener prefer this over asking ChatGPT directly?"
      If not — rewrite that section.
    
    Language: Ukrainian
    Tone: Confident, opinionated, intellectually engaging, conversational

---

## Output

A final podcast script that is:
- Structurally strong (hooks, retention, clear narrative arc)
- Intellectually alive (real opinions, real 12+ years experience)
- Timing-accurate (40-60 minutes)
- Ready for recording — used as a guide, not read word-for-word
